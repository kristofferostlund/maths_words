My name is Kristoffer Ã–stlund, and I am the author of this website. It's built mostly using plain JavaScript, HTML5 and CSS3, with the graphs being powered by D3.js. The repo can be found on my GitHub, right here if you fancy fiddling with it yourself.

I built this website to serve as a demonstration for a piece of coursework in a mathematics module at university. Though we've by no means have done any web development, I wanted to have an excuse to try out D3.js and do something cool with it.

This is really only one question, which consists of six parts:

  1. Count how many words there are in the article of different lengths and draw up a frequency table.
  2. Calculate the mode word length, the median word length and the mean word length from your frequency table.
  3. Which of the three averages in part (b) do you thnk is the most appropriate to use with your data and why?
  4. Draw up a cumulative frequency table and from this draw a cumualitve frequency polygon.
  5. Use the cumulative frequenct polygon to estimate the lower quartile and upper quartile.
  6. How representative of the English language, in terms of word length, do you think your sample is, and why?
